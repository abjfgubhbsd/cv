Verbal violence is the emergence of when we start to fight or we don't like it, also of aggression towards a classmate, being from another group or our own.

This emergence towards the person to whom the violence begins is also for being different from the others or just to bother. What would begin as verbal violence where we make fun of or begin to say angry things or insults can manifest itself in more ways, such as contempt, unpleasant blame and criticism towards the person as to humiliate, intimidate or control.

The longer this continues, the more serious effects the affected person could have on their mental and emotional health, having depression, shame, sadness and low self-esteem, losing their confidence and security, it would be difficult for them in their personal relationships. In addition, it can increase the risk of suffering from problematic substance use and eating disorders.

How could we avoid verbal violence, by not using strong and offensive language, it is better to speak respectfully, we should also speak with an older person who would be a teacher to be able to resolve the problem and avoid further verbal damage.
